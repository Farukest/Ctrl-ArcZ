Ctrl+ArcZ is a security layer for USDC payments on Arc. It ships as a TypeScript
SDK (`@ctrl-arcz/sdk`), a set of smart contracts, and reference apps. It protects
**both sides** of a payment: the recipient you send to, and you when you pay.

## The problem

The most effective attack on stablecoin users is not a smart-contract exploit. It
is **address poisoning**. An attacker seeds your transaction history with a
lookalike address, one that shares the same first and last visible characters as
someone you actually pay, by sending you a zero-value or dust "bait" transfer.
Later you copy the wrong address from your own history and send real funds to it,
on purpose.

The folk defense, "send $1 first and wait for it to arrive," is broken: it is slow,
it teaches nothing when the lookalike happens to be a live address, and it does
nothing about who can see your wallet when you pay. Ctrl+ArcZ replaces the ritual
with four layers that actually hold.

## The four layers

1. **Firewall (refuse to send).** A pre-send scan that refuses a lookalike or a
   zero-value-bait recipient before any funds move. This is the real defense
   against poisoning, because in a poisoning attack you send to the wrong address
   deliberately, so only refusing to send helps. Fail-closed by default.
2. **Protected transfer (code-gated claim).** Escrowed transfers the recipient
   claims with a short code, with sender `cancel` and automatic refund of an
   expired, unclaimed send. A genuinely mistaken send stays recoverable.
3. **Clean history.** Verified recipients and code-claimed settlement keep your
   activity spam-free and feed known-good addresses back into the firewall, so a
   lookalike of someone you have paid is caught too.
4. **Payer-side shield (Private Pay).** Pay a merchant from a disposable,
   policy-bound address that stores no payer identity, with every spend authorized
   by an enclave co-signer that vetoes a drainer, a lookalike, or an over-policy
   spend.

## Why Arc

Ctrl+ArcZ is built for Arc because Arc is what makes it practical:

- **USDC is the gas token.** Ephemeral payment addresses need no separate gas
  funding, the detail that kills this design on other chains.
- **Sub-second finality.** The create-fund-pay chain finishes in one breath, so a
  private checkout feels instant.
- **Circle tooling built in.** CCTP and Gateway move USDC in and out; Arc Privacy
  Sector (APS) is the roadmap for confidential balances.
