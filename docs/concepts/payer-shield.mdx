The payer-side shield is a disposable virtual card for crypto. Three primitives:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Vault" icon="vault">
    Your main kasa. Holds USDC and funds disposable accounts. The funding leg is
    the one that becomes confidential under Arc Privacy Sector.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Disposable account" icon="id-card">
    A single-purpose address, locked to one merchant, one cap, one expiry. Cloned
    per payment at a deterministic address. It stores no payer identity: the owner
    is bound only through the CREATE2 salt.
  </Card>
  <Card title="The Machine" icon="robot">
    The enclave co-signer. Runs policy plus the firewall and vetoes bad spends by
    withholding its signature.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## The trust model

- **Outbound to a third party (the merchant): the co-signer authorizes each
  spend.** A spend needs only "The Machine" signature, not a signature from you.
  This is safe because the funds are hard-locked to the pre-set `target` and
  capped by `maxAmount`: the co-signer can never steal or redirect, it can only
  push the exact payment you set up when you created and funded the account. It
  refuses a spend that fails policy or the firewall, so a bad payment is
  physically impossible, not merely warned against.
- **Return to your own vault: gated by knowledge of the vault address.** The vault
  is stored only as a commitment (`vaultHash`); a sweep passes the vault address
  and the contract checks it against the commitment, so no signature is needed and
  the vault stays hidden until that call. After `expiry`, anyone who knows the
  vault can sweep. This is the liveness escape hatch: if the enclave ever goes
  dark, funds are never stranded, they sweep home.

You sign nothing blind. Your only on-chain action is a normal, readable USDC
transfer to fund the account; your wallet shows "Send X USDC to 0x...", never an
opaque hash.

The account is a **one-way valve**: funds can only reach the locked merchant or
the vault. There is no third exit.

## What a veto stops

Because the target is locked on-chain, a script cannot redirect a payment. The
co-signer adds the dynamic layer: it will not sign for a target the firewall
flags as a drainer or a poisoning lookalike, an amount over the cap, or an expired
window. In whole-wallet mode the same veto can gate every outflow, not just
payments.

## Privacy, honestly

The **account surface leaks nothing about you**. There is no `owner` variable and
no `owner()` getter, so a merchant paid from the ephemeral address cannot read who
funded it from the account; the deployment calldata and the `AccountCreated` event
carry only `ownerHash` (indexed), not your address; and the vault is revealed only
at the moment of a sweep, and only to whoever already knows it. That part is fully
delivered now.

What is **not** yet hidden is the funding transaction. Your wallet to the
ephemeral account is a normal transfer on a transparent chain, so an observer can
still trace you to the ephemeral address through that one transaction. This cannot
be hidden today without confidential transfers. So the honest line is: the account
itself reveals nothing about you, but the residual on-chain link is the funding
transaction, which becomes confidential under Arc Privacy Sector. Full payer
privacy (hiding the funding link and amounts) waits on APS. The security legs
(policy, veto, sweep, one-way valve) are fully real now.

<Info>
**Roadmap: Arc Privacy Sector (APS).** APS is Arc's confidential execution
environment (TEE-based, running alongside the public EVM). When it ships, the
funding leg and account state move into APS and become confidential, and the same
co-signer speaks the same language as Arc's own privacy backend. The contracts are
architected for this: custody behind an adapter, getter-first reads (not
event-dependent), and clean function-level access boundaries.
</Info>

## Beyond one-off payments

The same account with `MODE_PULL` expresses recurring, policy-bounded spending:

- **Safe allowances / subscriptions** — a merchant can pull up to a cap on an
  interval, every pull gated by the co-signer. A `perPullMax` field caps each
  single pull: a subscription sets it to the per-period amount, so even a
  misbehaving co-signer cannot drain the whole cumulative cap in one pull. That
  per-pull cap is the real subscription guarantee. Kill the subscription by
  sweeping the account; the co-signer stops signing. No infinite-approval drainer.
- **Agent wallets** — hand an agent a tightly-scoped disposable account; it
  transacts autonomously but cannot exceed policy, because the enclave vetoes.
