Ctrl+ArcZ is a security layer for USDC payments on Arc. It covers **both sides** of
a payment: it protects the recipient you send to (address poisoning), and it
protects you when you pay (doxxing and drainers). It ships as a TypeScript SDK, a
set of smart contracts, and reference apps.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Private Pay" icon="user-secret" href="/guides/private-pay">
    Pay a merchant from a fresh, single-use address that stores no payer identity,
    guarded by an enclave co-signer that vetoes bad spends.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Poisoning firewall" icon="shield-halved" href="/guides/firewall">
    A pre-send scan that refuses a lookalike or zero-value-bait recipient before
    any funds move. Fail-closed by default.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Protected transfer" icon="lock" href="/guides/protected-transfer">
    Escrowed, code-claimed transfers: a wrong or poisoned send is recoverable,
    and the recipient claims with a short code.
  </Card>
  <Card title="The Machine (co-signer)" icon="robot" href="/guides/co-signer">
    The enclave that authorizes every spend. It does not warn, it vetoes: a spend
    that fails policy or the firewall is physically impossible, because it just
    withholds the signature.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Why Arc

Ctrl+ArcZ is built for Arc because Arc is what makes it practical:

- **USDC is the gas token.** Ephemeral payment addresses need no separate gas
  funding, the detail that kills this design on other chains.
- **Sub-second finality.** The create-fund-pay chain finishes in one breath, so a
  private checkout feels instant.
- **Circle tooling built in.** CCTP and Gateway move USDC in and out; Arc Privacy
  Sector (APS) is the roadmap for confidential balances.
